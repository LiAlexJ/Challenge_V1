Howard Hughes

From Aviator to Real Estate Mongul

  • 1905-Born in Houston, Texas on Christmas Eve. Father was a notable businessman who invented a rotary bit for oil well drilling that made the family extremely wealthy
  • 1916-Built Houston's first "wireless" radio transmitter at the age of 11.
  • 1917-Started taking math and engineering courses at CalTech. During his 20's, Hughes gave up his dreams to be a professional golfer to pursue other interests. Enrolls at Rice University
  • 1922-1924-Quit school to take over his father's business, Hughes Tool Company, in Houston.
  • 1926-Moved to Hollywood to enter the film industry. His several movies were known for being grossly over-budget and afoul of censors
  • 1928-Produced Two Arabian Knights, receiving the first "Academy Award for Best Director of a Comedy Picture".
  • 1930-Produced Hell's Angels, receiving an "Academy Award for Best Cinematography"
  • 1932-Founded Hughes Aircraft Company in Culver City, CA
  • 1935-In an airplane of his own design, sets the world's landplane speed record of 352.46 mph
  • 1937-Sets new transcontinental flight-time record to 7h:28mm
  • 1938-Circles Earth in a record time, 91h:14mm. Buys a share of Trans World Airlines(TWA), which he eventually acquired 78 percent of its stock
  • 1948-Buys RKO Pictures Corporation only to sell the company in 1955
  • 1953-Establishes the Howard Hughes Medical Institute, one of the world's largest charities
  • 1950s-Transformed Las Vegas into a more refined cosmopolitan city by buying casinos and large swathes of undeveloped land
  • 1957-Leaves the film industry. Had numerous relationships with prominent actresses. Married Jean Peters. Couple divorced in 1971
  • 1972-Sold Hughes Tool Company
  • 1976-Died on a flight from Acapulco, Mexico, to Houston Texas

"I intend to be the greatest golfer in the world, the finest film producer in Hollywood, the greatest pilot in the world, and the richest man in the world."-Howard Hughes
